Focaccia

Ingredients

  • 7g Active dry yeast
  • 780g Bread flour
  • 17g Salt
  • ¾ Cup Olive Oil
  • Water
  • Toppings (for example, rosemary, garlic & red pepper flakes)

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk 7g dry yeast with ½ cup warm water. Let sit for five minutes.
  2. After five minutes add 2 ½ cups of room temperature water, 780g sifted bread flour and 17g salt. Mix to incorporate the water with the flour (about five minutes). Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a damp, clean kitchen cloth. Let the dough rest for ten minutes.
  3. After ten minutes, mix / knead until the dough becomes very smooth and elastic (about 15 minutes with a mixer or 30 minutes if mixing by hand). Bread is more about feel than time.
  4. After mixing, pour ¼ cup olive oil into the bowl to cover the entire dough (including the bottom). Cover the bowl again with plastic wrap or towel, let the dough rest for 1 ½ - 2 hours. Dough should roughly double in size. Remember, bread is more about feel than time.
  5. Poke test the dough for readiness. Stick a finger into the dough. It should not immediately close up and instead roughly maintain its form.
  6. Oil a baking sheet tray with ¼ cup olive oil. Before transferring dough to a sheet tray, perform four quarter folds. Then, add dough to the sheet tray and gently stretch it to fill the tray. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Prepare toppings, mix with ¼ cup olive oil. For example, slice garlic and rosemary and marinate in olive oil with red pepper flakes.
  8. Remove plastic wrap and finish stretching dough into corners of sheet tray. Cover again with plastic wrap, let the dough rest in the refrigerator for 8-12 hours (overnight is great).
  9. After letting the dough rest, prepare for baking. Preheat the oven to 450 F. Remove dough from fridge, dimple the dough as if playing the piano to remove air bubbles. Apply toppings. Sprinkle coarse salt.
  10. After letting the oven preheat for thirty minutes, bake focaccia on the middle rack for 20 minutes. After twenty minutes, move the focaccia to the top rack and bake for another five minutes or until golden.
  11. Remove from the oven, let cool on a wire rack. Enjoy!

Mutter Paneer

Ingredients

  • Neutral Oil
  • 2-4 Green Cardamoms
  • 3 Garlic Cloves (Minced)
  • 1 Inch Ginger (Minced)
  • 1 Cup Onions (Cubed)
  • 1 ½ Cup Tomatoes (Chopped)
  • 12 Cashew Nuts
  • Salt
  • ⅓ Cup Yogurt
  • ¼ Teaspoon Cinnamon
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • ¼ Teaspoon Turmeric
  • ½ Teaspoon Chili Powder
  • 1 Teaspoon Garam Masala
  • 1 Teaspoon Coriander Powder
  • 1 Cup Frozen Green Peas
  • 250 Grams Paneer (Cubed)
  • 1 Teaspoon Dried Fenugreek Leaves
  • Fresh Chopped Cilantro

Instructions

  1. Heat one tablespoon of oil in a pan. When oil turns hot add green cardamoms, garlic and ginger. Fry for a minute.
  2. Add onions and saute until they turn light gold.
  3. Add tomatoes and ½ teaspoon salt. Saute for two to three minutes then add cashews. Cover and cook until mushy, five to ten minutes.
  4. Turn off heat. Let mixture cool then blend with yogurt to make a smooth puree.
  5. Heat another tablespoon of oil in the same pan. Add cinnamon and bay leaf. Let sizzle in oil for a minute, stir to not burn.
  6. Lower the heat then add turmeric, chili powder, garam masala and coriander powder. Mix well.
  7. Carefully add pureed onion and tomato mixture. Saute until the masala comes together and thickens. Stir frequently to prevent burning.
  8. Next, rinse the frozen peas and add to masala. Add water if necessary. Mix well to form a thick gravy.
  9. Add crushed, dried fenugreek and paneer. Cook until desired texture. Taste, adjust seasonings to your preference.
  10. Serve mutter paneer with basmati rice or naan. Enjoy!
